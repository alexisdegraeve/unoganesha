Welcome to the GUNO GAME

Goal of the Game

The goal of Uno is to be the first player to get rid of all your cards. The first player to play all their cards wins the game.

Setting Up the Game

  • Each player starts with 7 cards.
  • The remaining cards form a draw pile. The top card of the draw pile is turned over to start the discard pile.

Cards and How They Work

There are different colored and numbered cards, as well as special action cards:

  • Number Cards: Cards numbered from 0 to 9 in four colors (Red, Yellow, Green, Blue).
  • Action Cards:
    • Skip: The next player loses their turn.
    • Reverse: Reverses the direction of play.
    • Draw Two: The next player must draw 2 cards and lose their turn.
  • Wild Cards:
    • Wild Card: The player chooses the color to play.
    • Wild Draw Four: The player chooses the color and forces the next player to draw 4 cards.

How to Play

  • Players take turns. On each turn, a player must play a card that matches the color or number of the card on top of the discard pile.
  • If a player cannot play a card, they must draw a card from the draw pile. If the drawn card can be played, they play it immediately. If not, their turn ends.

The "Uno " Call

When a player has only one card left, they must call out "Uno! " before playing their last card. If a player forgets and another player catches it, they must draw two cards as a penalty.

To remind you to say "Uno ", a button will appear when you have two cards left. If you do not click it to call "Uno " when required, you will draw a card as a penalty.

End of the Game

The game ends when a player gets rid of all their cards. They score points based on the cards left in the other players' hands.
